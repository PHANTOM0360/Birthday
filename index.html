A Birthday Journal

"For the woman who made everywhere feel like home."

with love & memory  ·  Ebu

01   The Beginning
home, always
The earliest memory

You were the first warmth I ever knew.

Long before I could make sense of the world, I had already learned you — the steadiness in your hands, the calm in your voice, the way a whole room seemed to exhale when you walked in.

02   Growing Up
lessons without words
All the years between

You taught me things no classroom ever could.

How to be patient when patience felt impossible. How to keep going when everything inside you wanted to stop. How to love someone completely, without keeping score. You never made it a lesson — you just lived it, and I was watching the whole time.

03   A Still Life
ordinary, extraordinary

"The small moments were never actually small."

The morning routines. The meals we made noise over. The car rides with the window cracked just right. I didn't know, back then, that I was collecting them — holding them without realising. I know now. I'm grateful I wasn't paying less attention.

04   The Quiet Ones
quiet evenings
In the silence

The things you never said out loud were somehow the loudest.

A hand placed gently on a shoulder at just the right moment. A light left on past midnight, just in case. A meal kept warm without being asked. Your love never needed much volume. It had a way of filling the whole room anyway.

05   Strength
unbreakable
What I admire most

I have watched you carry the heaviest things and still stand tall.

You never let the hard parts swallow you. You bent — God, there were times you really bent — but you never broke. That quiet, stubborn resilience you carry? It lives in me too, now. It's the best thing I've inherited. I take it with me everywhere I go.

06 – 10   More of Us
A Letter
This birthday, and every one after.
Dear Maa,

I've been searching for the right words for longer than I'd like to admit. The truth is, there aren't any big enough. Language, just this once, isn't enough.

You gave me a sense of belonging before I even knew what that word meant. You made a home out of every place we lived — not with things, but with the way you moved through rooms. Steady. Warm. Certain. Like you'd already decided everything was going to be okay, and made me believe it too.

I want you to know that I noticed everything. Every early morning. Every late night you stayed up when you didn't have to. Every worry you held quietly to yourself so I wouldn't have to carry it. Every sacrifice you made without once calling it one.

You are fifty years of grace. Fifty years of love that never once kept a tally. And somehow — impossibly — you're still finding ways to give more.

Thank you. For the patience you gave me when I didn't deserve it. For the kindness you showed me when I was being difficult. For being present — really, fully present — in a way that I now understand is rarer than it should be.

Today belongs to you. Every page of this does. Every memory I held here was held with care — because you are worth that care, and a great deal more besides.

With all my love,
Ebu ♡

One more thing

There is a page I saved just for today.

Fifty years, and still the most radiant person in every room.

This isn't an ending — it's a note in the margin of something that carries on and on.

May this year bring you rest, real rest, and every small pleasure you've spent so long giving away to everyone else.

You deserve the whole world. You always did.
